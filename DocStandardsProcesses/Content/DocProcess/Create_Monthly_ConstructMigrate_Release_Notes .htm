Create Monthly Construct and Migrate Release Notes

Syniti Construct and Syniti Migrate release monthly and have a list of tickets to be completed. The standup meetings discuss the list of open tickets and progress of development work. As each ticket transitions from New through to Dev/Test status for development, a Technical Writer is assigned to the ticket and the ticket is worked for documentation.

If the ticket has no requirement for documentation, it is changed to status of Not Required and the Technical Writer field will be made blank.

The process for completing the documentation is defined in the Update Online Help section of Document Processes.

During the month, since these updates are not yet released to the public, they are assigned a Conditional Tag to hide them. These pages are also set as not searchable.

Monthly Cadence

  1. TW writes content based on Jira tickets in TEST status for these projects, has them reviewed by PM, and adds this content to the project using a CT called NotYetReleased.

  2. The TW keeps track of all changes made. Link the tickets in an epic (Ex: [MONTHNAME] Construct Migrate Docs).

  3. The TW adds approved content to the project throughout the month, using the CT to hide it.

  4. At the end of the month, the primary TW is @mentioned in a Jira ticket (ex: [DSP70-1924] Deploy Latest Construct build to Production - Syniti JIRA (atlassian.net)) that indicates the push to prod. This ticket tells CloudOps to push the production version of the products to the server.

  5. The TW responds on the ticket by confirming that the help is being pushed to prod.

    NOTE: If the primary writer doesn’t respond within a few hours, the secondary TW is @mentioned. If the secondary TW does not respond in a few hours, send an email to Dylan/Jenny.

  6. The TW removes the CTs from that month’s content, then rebuilds the SKP help on the dev and prod sites.

  7. Once complete, the TW adds a confirmation that the help is ready in production.

NOTE: During this phase

  • Syniti Construct and Syniti Migrate pages are searchable.

  • Updates to Construct and Migrate display on the What’s New released to the public.

  • Updates to Construct and Migrate are shared in the internal dkp_releases Slack channel, along with the other changes.
